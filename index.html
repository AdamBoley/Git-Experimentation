The octopus (plural octopuses) is a soft-bodied, eight-limbed mollusc of the order Octopoda.

Octopuses defend themselves against predators using the expulsion of ink, the use of camouflage and threat displays, the ability to jet quickly through the water and hide, and even deceit.

Octopuses inhabit various regions of the ocean, including coral reefs, pelagic waters, and the seabed; some live in the intertidal zone and others at abyssal depths. Most species grow quickly, mature early, and are short-lived.
